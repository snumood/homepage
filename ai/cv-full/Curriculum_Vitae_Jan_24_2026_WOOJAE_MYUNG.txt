A. Personal Statement

I am a clinical psychiatrist specializing in mood disorders and geropsychiatry, with a background in genomics
and bioinformatics. My research focuses on understanding the etiology of neuropsychiatric diseases through
data-driven approaches, integrating genetic, observational data, and public data sources.

My work has centered on predicting antidepressant responses using genetic data. A key early achievement was
investigating ethnic differences in genetic variants affecting antidepressant response, leading to the first Korean
genome-wide association study in this field. My research extends beyond genetic associations with depression
to include protein and RNA expression analyses. Collaborating with international consortia, such as the
Psychiatric Genomics Consortium (PGC), has allowed me to broaden the scope of my investigations.

Additionally, I have conducted genome-wide association studies (GWAS) focusing on novel mental health
phenotypes such as creativity, irritability, metabolic syndrome and occupational attainment, identifying genetic
variants linked to these traits and their connections to psychiatric disorders. I have significantly contributed to
enhancing genomic data from East Asian populations through cross-ancestry studies on psychiatric disorders,
educational attainment, and subjective well-being. I have also established an independent cohort of psychiatric
patients, including deep phenotyping of approximately 2,000 individuals and genotyping data for over 1,300
patients and 5,000 control subjects supporting global research collaborations to advance our understanding of
psychiatric conditions. As Director of the Precision Medicine Center at Seoul National University Bundang
Hospital, I am leading efforts to establish a hospital EMR-based biobank cohort and promote genomic research
to advance precision medicine.
As a clinical researcher, I have investigated emerging treatment and diagnostic modalities such as transcranial
direct current stimulation (tDCS), functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS), and digital therapeutics through
clinical trials. I have also worked on developing new clinical measurements for psychiatric assessment and
conducted network analyses to identify core symptoms, alongside MRI imaging studies in depressive patients.
Currently, I am researching the use of quantitative EEG (qEEG) for diagnosing mood disorders, with a focus on
perinatal and postpartum depression.

Leveraging the increasing availability of observational health data and social media platforms, I have conducted
extensive research into neuropsychiatric disease risk factors, including psychological distress, suicide, and
dementia, using large-scale national datasets such as the Korean Genome and Epidemiology Study and the
Korea National Health Insurance database. Integrating environmental data, I have examined how environmental
factors influence neuropsychiatric conditions, identifying associations with a range of disorders. My work with
the Global Burden of Disease consortium further contributes to understanding the global impact of these
conditions.
